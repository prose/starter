Although the official standalone link (http://www.google.com/chrome/eula.html?standalone=1) is normally outdated, you can grab the latest standalone installer from other sites.

They are available from cnet.com (http://download.cnet.com/Google-Chrome/3000-2356_4-10881381.html) and Softpedia (http://www.softpedia.com/get/Internet/Browsers/Google-Chrome.shtml).

To verify your installer, you'll need an MD5 checksum utility. You can grab a free one called WinMD5Free at http://www.winmd5.com. Simply drag the installer file to the program window and compare the MD5 string to the ones below.
